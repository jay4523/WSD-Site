Jared Cotter, an American Idol "Top 16" finalist was introduced to the world on the ever-popular TV show in 2007. Although, before he made his prime-time debut, Cotter was no newbie to the music industry. Jared had self-released remixes of "Make U Cry" and the Notorious B.I.G. inspired "Somebody's Gotta Go". Cotter even sang on "106 & Park". After American Idol, his career had hit a new high. "Fuse TV" had immediately picked him up as a host in the summer of 2007. Jared hosted three different shows for Fuse and quickly became a TV personality.

International superstar, Jay Sean invited Cotter to sit down and co-write his U.K. album in 2008. Jared happily obliged and was successful enough to co-write a majority of his U.S. release as well. With Billboard top 10 hits such as "Down" and "Where Them Girls At", it is difficult to deny Jared Cotter's momentum. Jared now currently holds a publishing deal at EMI Records, hosts "MSG Varsity's The Challenge" in New York and continues to write for artists like Jay Sean and Flo-Rida.
